## 3. Event Overview

The **FCAJ Community Day - Conference Call** gathered technology enthusiasts, students, and industry professionals to share practical knowledge about Artificial Intelligence, AWS cloud services, large language models, and enterprise software development. Throughout the event, speakers introduced real-world experiences, technical insights, and product development stories that demonstrated how modern technologies are applied in business environments.

### 09:00 - 09:30 AM: Context Is Everything – Making AI Actually Work for You

The opening presentation emphasized that providing sufficient context is essential for obtaining accurate and useful responses from AI systems. The speaker explained how AI performance depends on contextual information rather than prompts alone and introduced the concept of building a **Second AI Brain** through memory and structured knowledge. Practical suggestions were also provided to help learners interact with AI more effectively during both study and software development.

### 09:30 - 10:00 AM: 36 Hours with LotusHacks – Building UTMorpho from Idea to Reality

This session shared the complete journey of a hackathon team that created **UTMorpho** within only 36 hours. The speakers described how the initial idea was formed, how user problems were identified, and how the solution evolved under strict time constraints. They also discussed technical challenges, teamwork experiences, and lessons learned throughout the competition before presenting a demonstration of the final product.

### 10:00 - 10:40 AM: From Edge to Origin – CloudFront as Your Foundation

The presentation introduced **Amazon CloudFront** as an important component for building scalable and secure cloud applications. Topics included content delivery optimization, reducing operational costs, improving application security, increasing service reliability, and delivering faster responses to users located in different geographic regions.

### 10:40 - 10:55 AM: Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Quick

This session demonstrated several AI-powered tools within the Amazon Quick ecosystem that simplify data analysis and collaboration. The speaker introduced:

- **Quick Chat Agent** for conversational data analysis.
- **Quick Flows** for creating intelligent workflows using natural language.
- **Quick Spaces** for sharing organizational knowledge.
- **Quick Sight** for generating reports and dashboards from raw data with AI assistance.

### 10:55 - 11:00 AM: Break

Participants had a short networking break before the final technical sessions.

### 11:00 - 11:30 AM: Non-Determinism of "Deterministic" LLM Settings

This presentation explained why Large Language Models may still generate different outputs even when configured with deterministic settings such as `temperature = 0`. The speaker discussed how token prediction works, the influence of inference optimizations, and why identical inputs do not always guarantee identical outputs. Several practical techniques for reducing inconsistency when deploying AI systems were also presented.

### 11:30 AM - 12:00 PM: Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System – Startup Credit Scoring Case Study

The final session focused on designing enterprise-level **multi-agent systems** through a startup credit scoring scenario. The presentation explained the limitations of traditional banking evaluation methods when dealing with startup businesses and demonstrated how multiple AI agents can collaborate as a **Virtual Credit Committee**. Additional topics included system architecture, governance, compliance, deployment strategy, and long-term business value.

## 4. Reflection and Learning Outcomes

Participating in this event provided valuable insights that closely relate to my internship project and future career development.

One of the most valuable lessons came from the presentation about **AI context engineering**. It highlighted that successful AI applications rely not only on advanced models but also on well-structured contextual information. This idea directly applies to my AWS-based fraud detection project, where carefully designed transaction features and preprocessing pipelines are essential for producing reliable predictions.

The session introducing **Amazon CloudFront** broadened my understanding of cloud architecture by demonstrating how performance, scalability, and security can be improved through content delivery optimization. Although CloudFront is not directly used in my current project, these concepts are highly relevant for deploying APIs and user-facing services in production environments.

Another important takeaway was the discussion about **LLM non-determinism**. Understanding that AI systems may still produce different outputs under seemingly identical settings emphasized the importance of evaluation, logging, monitoring, and guardrail mechanisms, particularly in financial applications where consistency and reliability are critical.

Finally, the presentation on **multi-agent systems for credit scoring** offered a broader perspective on applying AI in financial services. Beyond prediction accuracy, enterprise AI solutions must also provide transparency, compliance, and explainability. These ideas will be useful references when extending the architecture and future capabilities of my AWS Fraud Detection system.

As a participant, I attended every technical session, documented the key ideas presented by each speaker, and reflected on how these concepts relate to my internship project. The knowledge gained from this event will support the improvement of my internship report, especially in the areas of cloud architecture, AI system reliability, and future solution design.
